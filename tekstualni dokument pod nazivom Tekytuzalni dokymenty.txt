A letter of 5. November 1777 to Mozart's cousin Maria Anna Thekla Mozart is an example of Mozart's use of scatology. 
The German original is in rhymed verse.


Well, I wish you good night
But first shit in your bed and make it burst.
Sleep soundly, my love
Into your mouth your arse you'll shove.


Mozart's canon "Leck mich im Arsch" K. 231 (K6 382c) includes the lyrics:

Leck mich im A[rsch] g'schwindi, g'schwindi!

This would be translated into English as "lick me in the arse/ass, quickly, quickly!"

"Leck mich im Arsch" is a standard vulgarism in German, euphemistically called the Swabian salute (German: schw‰bische Gruﬂ).
The closest English counterpart is "Kiss my arse/ass".
